Inversions

When the inverted words form a single lemma

The sign-sequence is to be entered as it appears in the script.

When the inverted words do not form a single lemma

Each word is to be encoded with the signs that belong to it. The inverted sign-sequence cannot be reproduced/depicted.

When the inversion occurs while the word boundaries are broken

E.g. The signs have to be assigned to words they belong to (in this case V30+G7 and I9 separately). The inverted sign-sequence cannot be reproduced/depicted with the hieroglyphs but the program offers an option to mark such inversions.

For this purpose, "Select Singe Glyphs" function is available. In the example above with "nb=f", you would then select the G7 with arrows below the "Select Single Glyphs" section. The sign will appear red, but only for orientation purposes in the field above, where the word is displayed. You can then set the "Order in Sentence" to the position where the sign occurs in the manuscript (in this case, it appears on the position nr.7, after the I9 sign. Then do the same with the I9 sign, assigning desired position to it in the sentence (in this case nr.6). See also Hieroglyph Type Writer (HTW).
