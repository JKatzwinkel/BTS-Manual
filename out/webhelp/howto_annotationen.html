4. Annotations, Rubra, Glosses and Comments

An annotation can be a free annotation, a rubrum, a gloss or a comment by the translator. You can annotate a word or a sequence of words, a sentence or several sentences.

Mark the sequence you want to annotate (for only one word you just need to place the cursor somewhere in the word except the word is made of one character only: then you must place the cursor directly behind the character). In General, you can create more than one annotation for the same token. In Addition, you can create one (same) annotation for more than one token. NOTE: sometimes the buttons may be shown as inactive, click somewhere outside of your text and then again into it, so that it works. Now choose the right kind of annotation with the buttons above the Text Editor:

Add Annotation

Adds an Annotation in the active part of your text. It is indicated by a black bar on the right side of the text Editor. For this type of annotations there is the possibility to enter Passport Data. This is because you may want to give extratextual information for tokens or parts of the text you are entering.

Add Rubrum

Converts the active lemma on the word sequence into a rubrum, which is indicated by red script in the text and a red bar on the right. Note: At the moment you cannot delete rubra via the "Undo" button.

Add Subtext/Gloss

Adds a subtext or a gloss respectively as a "child" of your text in the corpora tree. You can edit it separately. The subtext will also appear on the left in the Corpora view under the chosen text.

Add Comment

Adds a comment to the selected part. It will be underlined yellow.

You can also use these functions via the drop-down menu that appears when you click the triangle to the right of the annotation symbols.

The existing annotations are displayed in the Annotation window on the right. A new annotation will appear at the bottom of this list. With the arrow on the right you can expand the annotation and see more information as well as further editing options.

Subtext/gloss will be edited like a regular text.

You can delete an annotation by selecting it and clicking Delete in the upper right corner of the Annotations tab. A confirmation window will appear, click Delete again to confirm your decision or Cancel if you change your mind.

What is the difference between an annotation, a subtext, a rubrum or a comment?

Annotation An annotation can be used to mark parts of a text with the intention to give extratextual information including bibliographical references using Thesaurus. Therefore annotations can include metadata. Annotations are not limited to one fragment, they can be connected with more than one section of a text or beyond. See Annotation and text translation window.

Examples: If you want to divide your text into chapters (e.g. first part being a biography and the next offering scene) then use annotations to mark them (the titles of particular sections cannot be inserted in the transliteration in the Text Editor).

Subtext use it to mark sections of a text as a subtext, e.g. in a case where the sequence of the chapters is not clear.

Rubrum act as special annotations and therefore you should use this instead of "Annotation" to mark rubra in the text.

Comment A comment is an information to a text which can discuss problems of a phrase or a word but cannot be related to other sections (unlike Annotations, comments cannot have metadata such as e.g. bibliographical information). A comment is a continuous text where you can insert anything you wish to say outside of the transliteration.
