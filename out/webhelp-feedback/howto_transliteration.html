1. Transliteration

To enter and edit the transliteration of your texts, click into the Text Editor window in the upper centre of the User Interface and enter your text. You first need to install and configure the keyboard, provided by BBAW.

Transliteration follows the BTS grammar rules. Most important rules are the following:

  • Start and end each sentence with the sentence marker "§"
  • Use space only in between two words or extratextual information, not after typing a sentence marker "§" or after opening brackets.
  • Brackets come in pairs. You have to close every bracket you opened. Sometimes the system will do this automatically, but there are cases where that does not work. Clicking "Strg+Space" will show the markers that are allowed at the position of the curser. For recurring elements you also can define a template in thePreferences.
  • The graphical dual and plural endings (both when ".w" or ".wj/tj" are written out or when they are just marked with dual/plural strokes or double/triple repetition of signs) are transcribed with ".du" and ".pl" respectively.
  • Ambiguities are entered as: "%case 1: rd.du| case 2: war.t.du%". Be careful not to type a space in front of the vertical stroke.
  • It is not allowed to leave out an ending ("nb."), to write two full stops ("nb.."), to close brackets before you open them ("n]b[") or to interlace different kinds of brackets wrongly ("{n[b}]").

Adding Extratextual Information:

  • Linecounts are introduced by #lc: and end with #. Ex.: Line 1 = #lc: [1]#
  • Paragraphs start with #para: and end with #. CT VI 106a = #para: CT VI 106a#
  • Destruction is indicated by two hyphens --...--: --rest of line destroyed--, --3Q-- (destruction of 3 scriptorial squares).
  • For all other information #...# is to be used.
  • If the number and/or kind of words missing can be determined, use "[___]" (underline instead. Ex.: "[___] [___]" for 2 missing words.
  • All other descriptive information is to be put between "#": Ex.: #in front of the first person looking left:#

Some transliteration conventions:

"," (comma) is used...

"." (dot) is used...
