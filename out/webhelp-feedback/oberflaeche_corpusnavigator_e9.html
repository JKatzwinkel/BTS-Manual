Corpus Navigator

The "Corpus Navigator" tab is located on the left side of the "Corpus" work space. Here you can see the position of the text in the corpus, displayed hierarchically. You can navigate through the corpus "tree" by clicking or using the "up" and "down" arrow keys on your keyboard.

Apart from the object title, the physical location of the object is displayed in the square brackets. The number of elements it contains is shown in round brackets.

"Corpus Navigator" has two tabs: "Text Corpora" and "Bin". By default the tab "Text Corpora" is activated. In the "Bin" tab you can find your deleted texts and objects. Please note that generating the list of the objects in the "Bin" tab may take up to one minute the first time you click it after installing BTS. Thereafter the preview works faster.

At the top of the "Corpus Navigator" window there are several buttons. Two of them: and - are also available in other windows and are therefore explained in the "Common features"- section.

"Corpus Navigator"-specific buttons are the following:

"New Corpus": Click to create a new corpus.

"Add Text Corpus Object": Adds a "child"-object to the currently selected object. You choose the active element with a click. NOTE: The physical location where the child element is saved is not the active corpus but your main working corpus you have choosen in the Preference Settings .

"addNewText": Adds a "child" text to the selected corpus. NOTE: The physical location where the child element is saved is not the active corpus but your main working corpus you have choosen in the Preference Settings .

"addAnnotation": Adds an annotation to active object.

"Delete": Moves the selected element from the "Corpus Navigator" tab to the "Bin "tab. In the "Bin" you can either to restore it or permantly delete it per right mouse-click.

"Open Simple Search Dialog": Open a search dialog.

Clicking on the triangle will display the drop-down menu with the following entries:

"Edit Updaters / Readers" and "Open revision history" share the functionality with the eponymous entries in the menu bar.

"Filter" - filters the corpora acoording to the listed criteria.

"Delete permanently" - permanantly deletes the selected object.

"Restore" - restores the selected object (moves it from "Bin" to "Corpus Navigator" tab).
