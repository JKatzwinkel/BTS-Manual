Special signs

Superfluous signs:

When a sign appears to be superfluous it is still to be encoded as found in the manuscript. The superfluous signs outside of a wordform are to be pragmatically assigned to the previous or the following wordform. This can be rendered with [{sign}]:

Dots:

The black dot is rendered by the capital letter 'O' and the red dot by the small letter 'o.

Cartouches:

Cartouche is an oval form surrounding a word: It is to be entered by "<>" + Hieroglyphs + ">"

"Serekhs":

A rectangular frame is to be entered with "<S" + Hieroglyphs + ">"

Hw.t-sign:

A sign enclosed in a rectangular enclosure (Gardiner's O6) is to be entered with "<H" + Hieroglyphs + ">"

Haplographies:

Haplographies are encoded normally at the position of the first occurrence. In the further occurrence they are to be encoded in double angle brackets: [&-[&-sign-&]-&] . E.g. in this case the sign X8 should be written twice, so it needs to be inserted into double angle brackets:
