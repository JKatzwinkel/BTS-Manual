4. Annotations, Rubra, Glossed and Comments

An annotation can be a free annotation, a rubrum, a gloss or a comment by the translator. You can annotate a word or a sequence of words, a sentence or several sentences.

Mark the sequence you want to annotate (for only one word you just need to place the cursor somewhere in the word. (NOTE: sometimes the buttons may be shown as inactive, click somewhere outside of your text and then again into it, so that it works). Now choose the right kind of annotation with the buttons above the Texteditor:

"Add Annotation" adds an Annotation in the active part of your text. It is indicated by a black bar on the right side of the text Editor.

"Add Rubrum" converts the active lemma into a rubrum, which is indicated by red script in the text and a red bar on the right. Note: At the moment you cannot delete lemmata via the "Undo" button.

"Add Subtext/Glosse" adds a subtext or glosse respectively as a "child" of your text. You can edit it separately.

"Add Comment" adds a comment to the selected part. It will be underlined yellow.

You can also use those functions via the drop-down menu that appears when you click the triangle to the right of the annotation symbols.

The existing annotations are displayed in the Annotation window on the right. A new annotation will appear at the bottom of this list. With the arrow on the right you can expand the annotation and see more information as well as further editing options. The annotation will also appear on the left in the Coprora view under the chosen text.

Subtext/gloss will be edited like a regular text.

You can delete an annotation by selecting it and clicking "Delete" in the upper right corner of the Annotations tab. A confirmation window will appear, click "Delete" again to confirm your decision or "Cancel" if you change your mind.
